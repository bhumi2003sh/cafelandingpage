Coffee Making Live

The live process of coffee making starts with selecting high-quality beans, freshly ground to release their aromas. Water is heated to 195°F-205°F and poured over the grounds in a filter or portafilter. As the coffee brews and drips into a cup, its rich aroma fills the air, resulting in a freshly rewed cup ready to be enjoyed for its full-bodied flavor and invigorating warmth.

Cappucino
A rich, frothy coffee drink made with equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam, often enjoyed with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or cinnamon.
Latte
A creamy coffee drink made with one shot of espresso and steamed milk, topped with a small amount of milk foam, creating a mild and smooth flavor.
Macchiato
An espresso with a small amount of steamed milk or milk foam added, resulting in a strong coffee flavor with a hint of creaminess.

A smooth and bold coffee beverage created by diluting

a shot of espresso with hot water with different flavor profile than drip coffee.

Mocha
A chocolate-flavored coffee made with espresso, steamed milk, and chocolate syrup, often topped with whipped cream and cocoa powder.
Flat White

Similar to a latte but with a higher ratio of coffee to milk, made with a shot of espresso

and micro-foamed milk, ating a velvety texture.

Expresso

A strong, concentrated coffee

brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans, serving as the base for many other coffee drinks and appreciated for its flavor.

Ristretto

A more concentrated and shorter version of an espresso, made with the same amount of coffee but less water, resulting in a

stronger and richer flavor.

Cold Brew
Made by steeping coarsely-ground coffee beans in cold water for an extended period, usually 12-24 hours, roducing a smooth, less acidic coffee served cold.
